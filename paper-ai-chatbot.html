Paper AI Build a tiny chatbot with a pencil, paper, and one number called memory.

A real AI chatbot has billions of numbers. Ours has one. We call it memory. The chatbot reads your words, mixes them into its memory, and then says the word whose secret number is closest to that memory. That's it. You can do every step with a calculator. Let's go. 👇

① The Rules (the whole brain)

Every word has a secret number between 0 and 1. The chatbot keeps one running number, its memory. Here is everything it knows how to do:

Encoder — reads your message

Start memory at 0.0. For each word you type, update:

new_memory = 0.5 × current + 0.5 × input_word_value

The final memory is the context number — a summary of your whole message.

Decoder — writes the reply

Start memory at the context number. The first "previous word" is <START>. Each step:

new_memory = 0.5 × current + 0.5 × previous_word_value

Then pick the word whose value is closest to memory. That word becomes the next "previous word."

Score — how good is a word?

For each possible word, the score is how close its value is to memory:

score = −| memory − word_value |

Closer = higher score (closer to 0). The highest score wins and gets spoken.

Tie-breakers — exact ties only
  • First word of the reply: prefer a real word over <END>.
  • Later words: prefer <END> (so the chatbot knows when to stop talking).

<END> is the "I'm done" word. When it wins, the reply is finished.

② The Vocabulary (every word's secret number)

These are the numbers we glued onto each word. You can change any of them — every table on this page updates instantly. (This is the "brain." Training just means picking good numbers.)

③ Training (teacher forcing)

To teach the chatbot a reply, we show it the right answer at every step (that's "teacher forcing"). At each step we make the full score table for every word and check: did the correct word get the highest score? If not, we nudge that word's number until it wins. That nudging is the training.
